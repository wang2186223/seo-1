Waylon, now absently adjusting the bouquet in his hands, gave her a side glance. "A casual apology like that? No way. That's something we'll need to talk about properly. And do you really want to air our private business in front of everyone?"

Alexia turned her head and realized a sizable crowd had formed, eyes wide with curiosity, whispers swirling around them like a breeze.

Without thinking twice, she grabbed Waylon's arm and dragged him away. "Let's jst go!"

Waylon didn't resist, letting her pull him along. A faint smile tugged at his lips. "By the way, I prefer irises over roses. Just keep that in mind next time."

Alexia shot him a look, her voice dry. "Then buy them yourself."

Nearby, a group of employees exchanged stunned glances, struggling to believe what they had just seen.

As Alexia and Waylon walked off together under the curious gaze of everyone around, it didn't take long for whispers to explode into full-blown gossip, filling the air with wild speculation.

"Did you see that? Mr. Mason actually accepted those flowers!"
