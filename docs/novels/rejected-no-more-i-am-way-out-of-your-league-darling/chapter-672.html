Chapter 668

"I might not have money, but I wasn't built for struggle. If your plans don't include me, then I've got no choice but to look after myself. Isn't that fair?"Caiden's voice turned sharp. "You're cold, Daniela.Always calculating. Can you really fault me for taking care of myself?"

He waved a hand toward the shelves. "And let's be honest. You can't have kids anymore. What's the point of keeping all that stuff your mother saved? The toys she bought for your future child-who's going to use them now? I might as well sell them and live a better life. What's the harm in that?"

He stepped closer, his tone rising. "I was meant for luxury. I'm your father. You're supposed to take care of me! Your mother made sure I lived well. You should do the same. I'm all you've got. There's no one else.Just me. In this world, I'm your only family. So, no matter what I do, you owe me. You hear me?"

Daniela didn't flinch. Her eyes stayed fixed on the television screen. Without looking his way, she said,"Is that what you believe?"

That one sentence wiped the smile off his face. "What's that supposed to mean? You'd better think hard before you act. I'm your father. I'm the closest person you've got. Are you really planning to turn on

me?"

Daniela didn't respond.

Just then, the front door creaked open.

The new director from the asylum stepped in and greeted Daniela. "Miss Harper."

Daniela didn't even lift her eyes. "Take my father with you. And this time, make sure he doesn't escape."

Caiden blinked. His jaw went slack. Then came the scream."No! Daniela! You can't do this!"

But it was already in motion. He was dragged through the hall, yelling so loud it shook the walls. The villa erupted in chaos.

Joyce froze where she stood. Her lips parted, but no sound came. She watched, too stunned to breathe.

Nikolas came down the stairs, confused. "What happened just now?"

Still watching the screen, Daniela spoke in a clipped voice. "He's lost his mind. He claimed he was my only family. So I did what anyone would do-l made sure he's looked after." She pushed herself off thne couch and walked to the kitchen without looking back.

Nikolas stood at the doorway, eyes following the corridor.

Through the open door, he saw Caiden being forced into a van, a cloth shoved in his mouth,arms bound tightly. The door slammed shut. The van sped off. His screams faded into the distance.

Joyce sank to the floor, completely overwhelmed.

Daniela didn't react. She picked up her bowl and spooned oatmeal into her mouth.

Nikolas watched her, a heavy feeling settling in his chest. She had sent her own father away, and she hadn't blinked once.

"Mr. McCoy, would you like something to eat?"Daniela asked, her tone even.

He looked toward the doorway, still trying to understand what he'd just seen. "You don't get along with your family?"

Without lifting her head, Daniela answered, "I don't have any family."

Nikolas asked, "What about Cedric?" Right then, Cedric stepped out of the room. He stopped in place when he caught the end of the conversation.

Daniela let out a small laugh. Nikolas glanced at her.watching closely.

Then she said in an easy tone, "Mr. McCoy, ever heard this one before?"

Nikolas tilted his head."What?"

She turned, her stare sharp enough to pierce."Curiosity killed the cat."

He didn't get a chance to respond before she added,"Same way those mercenaries ended up scattered that night."

Nikolas stiffened. The change in his face was immediate.

Then his eyes darkened, fixed on her with an unfamiliar chill.

A heavysilence fell over the room.

Just as Nikolas opened his mouth to say something,Tasha cut in. "Mr. Phillips? What would you like for breakfast?"

Daniela kept her eyes down and continued eating without saying a word.

Nikolas blinked, his focus drifting. Then he quietly joined her at the table.

Cedric stood to the side, watching both of them. He was certain if Tasha hadn't spoken when she did,Nikolas would've said whatever was brewing in his head.But in the end, Nikolas said nothing.

After breakfast, Daniela went upstairs without looking back.

Nikolas stepped out onto the porch to take a call.

"Is Cedric dead yet?" the voice asked.

"No," he answered flatly.

"What? Do you want us to help you?"

"Not right now."

"I thought you hired people to kill Cedric?"

Nikolas said nothing right away. His eyes wandered to a room on the second floor.

Daniela, with her remarkable insight, was definitely not as simple as she appeared.

She might become his biggest obstacle in dealing with Cedric.
