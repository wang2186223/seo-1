Nikolas stood rooted to the spot.

So that was it-his father saw him as nothing more than a backup, a replaceable pawn to be discarded at will?

Nikolas' vision blurred with rage, his breath sharp and erratic as fury surged through him.

Meanwhile, Daniela was reviewing construction budgets when a large delivery of food boxes arrived at the door.

She got up to investigate. The delivery man glanced at his clipboard and said, "Cedric Phillips placed the order."

"Cedric?" Her brow furrowed.

Cedric wasn't the type to bother with logistics like this. The delivery man fidgeted under her gaze, and the unease in Daniela's chest deepened.

Before she could press him further, a voice called out from the entryway. She turned to see Nikolas approaching.

"Daniela, got a minute?"

She cast one last look at the stack of food boxes,then addressed the delivery man firmly. "Hold off on bringing anything upstairs. I want to see the order receipt first."

With that, she pivoted on her heel and strode toward Nikolas, her expression tight.

As she neared him, she cast one last look over her shoulder. The delivery man was definitely digging through his phone for something.

Then she faced Nikolas. "What is it now?"

"Nothing big. Just thought we should have a quick word," he replied with an easy shrug.

But his gaze slid past her, landing on the delivery man in a subtle cue.

Catching it, the delivery man gave a small nod before murmuring to his partner, "Move."
